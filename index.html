More About Me

There are only so many hours in a day, and I think I’ve managed to fill all 30 of them.

My life and career have revolved around problem solving in one capacity or another. There is something supremely satisfying in that moment, after nearly imploding in frustration, when everything just … clicks.

First and foremost, as a husband of 23 years, and father of many wonderful children, ranging from post college to attending to not-sure-she-wants-to-be-yet, on down to my youngest, who has shown us how to meet every challenge with enthusiasm.

For twenty years, I labored in various forms of residential, commercial, and industrial building construction/ renovation. From installing windows, to tile, to foundation work, all the way to building a home and shipbuilding, nothing is ever as straightforward as the engineers drew it up.

More recently I have worked as both an Electrician and Maintenance Technician in the shipyard industry, servicing our nation’s Navy and Amphibious Forces. Currently, I am serving as Lockout Tagout Technician as well as Work Authorization Form Coordinator.

Throughout my career, I’ve demonstrated an ability to work with teams and individuals across disciplines as well as been trusted to work independently with little or no oversight. I have filled many roles at various times as a:

  • Production Team Leader
  • Lead Mechanic
  • Mentor and Tutor
  • Sole Proprietor, and Business Partner


I have recently earned a degree in Computer Programming through remote classes at College of the Albemarle, including studies in Visual Basic, Databases, and Information Systems.

My free time, what’s left after a 48-hour work week, family, and studies, is spent exploring C# and building small projects to further my skills and understanding.
